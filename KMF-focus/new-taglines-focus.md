## 30. Defend Your Time - focus

*Defend Your Time*

Time is the one resource you can't get back — treat every hour like it belongs to your future self, because it does. Defend it from distraction, obligation, and other people's urgency.

### Focus

## N34. One Tab Open - focus

*One Tab Open*

Every open tab argues for your attention — close everything except the one task. One tab open and attention has one place to land.

## N35. Finish The Switch - focus

*Close Before Opening*

Scattered focus leaves everything almost done — close one task before you open another.

## N36. Hold The Thread - focus

*Hold The Thread*

Interruptions feel small until your day is fragments — hold the thread on one task until it's done. One thread beats another day of busy nothing.

## N37. Make Depth The Default - focus

*Make Depth The Default*

Shallow work fills the calendar — block one hour for depth and protect it. Make depth the default and progress stops waiting for a free afternoon.

## N38. Cut The Ping - focus

*Cut The Ping*

Every ping trains you to drop what matters for what's loudest — cut it for one work block. Cut the ping and the task keeps your focus long enough to move forward.

## N39. Stay In The Lane - focus

*Stay In The Lane*

Jumping lanes feels productive until nothing finishes — pick one lane for this hour. Stay put and one lane actually gets done.

## N40. Name The One Thing - focus

*Name The One Thing*

A list of ten priorities is a list of zero — name the one thing that would make today count. One target beats a crowd of almost-urgent tasks.

## N41. Fence Your Focus - focus

*Wear The Blinders*

A wide-open day scatters attention — put on blinders for one hour with one tab and one task. Blinders on and the periphery stops pulling you off.

## N42. Close The Noise Door - focus

*Close The Noise Door*

Noise doesn't knock — it comes in through what you left open. Close the noise door before you work and your mind isn't pulled two ways from the start.

## N43. Prioritize Ruthlessly - focus

*Prioritize Ruthlessly*

Everything can't be urgent at once — pick the one outcome that would make today count. Ruthless priority turns a crowded list into one clear lane.

## N44. Do One Thing First - focus

*Do One Thing First*

Multitasking is five tasks at half attention — do one thing first. Finish the first pass and quality replaces the illusion of busy.

## N45. Finish Before Switching - focus

*Finish Before Switching*

Switching feels productive until nothing completes — finish before you switch. Done as the rule beats a day of fragments.
