# Drop The Story

*Drop The Story*

The narrative you keep telling about why you can't is the real obstacle — drop the story and you're left with just the situation, and situations are solvable. You're not your backstory; you're what you do next.

## Category

**Mindset** — A limiting narrative turns every problem into identity; *Drop The Story* answers "How do I change the way I think about this?" by separating what happened from what you do next.

## The idea

The story you tell about why you cannot — wrong background, wrong timing, wrong town — feels like an explanation, but it functions like a lock. Drop the narrative and what remains is a situation, and situations respond to action in ways that stories never do. You are not your backstory. You are the next decision you make.

## Story

You have told the same story for years, and it is a good one. Wrong city. Wrong degree. Wrong break at the wrong time. It is detailed enough to sound like analysis and familiar enough that you can recite it without thinking. Every new idea dies the same death: that works for other people, not someone with your history.

The story protects you from risk. It also protects you from effort, because if the outcome is predetermined by circumstances, then trying hard is just setting yourself up for disappointment. You have been living inside that logic so long you forgot it was a choice.

A mentor interrupts mid-rant. "What if the story is not true — what if it is just comfortable?" You want to argue. Instead you go quiet and spend a week catching yourself mid-sentence. Not replacing the old story with affirmations, just refusing to let it run on autopilot.

What is left is almost boring. You need a portfolio, so you build one. You need introductions, so you send five emails. You need a skill, so you enroll in a night class. None of that required a different childhood or a luckier zip code. It required stopping the narrative long enough to see the actual problem, which turns out to be solvable.

Three months later someone calls your trajectory inspiring. You almost laugh. You did not overcome your past. You stopped letting it write your future.

## Takeaway

The story about why you cannot is often more comfortable than the work of finding out whether you can. Drop it, and you are left with something rarer than motivation — a clear view of what actually needs to happen next.
