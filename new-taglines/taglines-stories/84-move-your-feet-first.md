# Move Your Feet First

*Move Your Feet First*

Your feelings often follow your feet, not the other way around. Move your feet first and let mood catch up to motion.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting to feel ready keeps you standing still; *Move Your Feet First* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by letting action lead and allowing emotion to arrive afterward.

## The idea

The common assumption is that motivation comes first and action follows — that you need to feel inspired, rested, or confident before you can begin. Most days disagree. The body can move while the mind is still negotiating, and movement often changes what the mind is willing to feel. The challenge is trusting your feet more than your mood. When you move first — one step, one rep, one errand toward the thing you are avoiding — you give feeling something to attach to instead of asking it to lead from a standstill.

## Story

Marcus woke up on Tuesday with the same gray weight he had carried for weeks. The garage gym was ten feet from his bed. He had been telling himself he would train when he felt like himself again.

He put his feet on the floor before he finished the thought. Shoes, water, one plate on the bar. He did not wait to feel energized. He loaded the bar because his hands were already there.

The first set felt mechanical. The second set felt slightly less like a punishment. By the third he was breathing harder and noticing the room instead of the story in his head about how hard everything was.

He did not leave the garage transformed. He left having moved, and movement had done what waiting could not — it gave his mood something recent and physical to reference. Later that morning he returned an email he had avoided for three days. His feet had gone first. The rest of him had followed.

## Takeaway

Stop requiring the right feeling before the first step. Move your body toward the task and give your mood a chance to catch up to what your feet already know how to do.
