# Write The Ugly First Line

*Write The Ugly First Line*

The first line does not have to sing — it has to exist. Write the ugly opening and give the rest of the page something to follow.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for a perfect opening keeps the page blank indefinitely; *Write The Ugly First Line* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by putting any first sentence on the page so the rest of the work has something to build from.

## The idea

A blank page feels demanding because the first line seems to set the standard for everything after it. You hunt for something clever, true, and strong enough to justify the project — and the hunt becomes the project. The ugly first line breaks that trap. It does not announce your talent. It announces that writing has begun. Once something is on the page, you have material to revise, reject, or follow. The rest of the piece rarely appears until the first line stops being theoretical.

## Story

Naomi had been assigned to write the opening chapter of her company's annual report — the human story behind a year of numbers. She stared at the blinking cursor for an hour on Monday. She tried three elegant openings in her head and rejected all of them before they reached the screen.

By Tuesday she was behind and annoyed with herself. At lunch she read an article that said writers who wait for a beautiful first sentence often never get to the second. She closed the article, opened the document, and typed: "This year was harder than we expected."

It was plain. It was not the line she would keep. It was a line.

She wrote the next sentence about a product launch that slipped. Then a paragraph about a team that stayed late through a rough quarter. The ugly opener gave her somewhere to go. By four o'clock she had six pages and one opening she actually liked — which she found by rewriting the blunt sentence she had been too proud to write on Monday.

The chapter shipped on time. The first line in the final draft was nothing like her ugly start. The ugly start was the reason a final draft existed at all.

## Takeaway

When the page stays blank because the opening is not good enough yet, type the plainest true sentence you can and keep going. You are not writing the final first line. You are writing the one that makes the second line possible.
