# Take The Hit

*Take The Hit*

Life is going to land punches — the question is whether you stay down or absorb it and keep going. Taking the hit without quitting is how ordinary people become unstopable

## Category

**Resilience** — A setback can feel like a full stop; *Take The Hit* answers "How do I keep going when it gets hard?" by absorbing the blow without treating it as permission to quit.

## The idea

Life will land punches you did not schedule and cannot always dodge — a rejection, a loss, a setback that arrives without warning. The question is never whether you get hit; it is whether you stay down or absorb the blow and keep moving. When you take the hit without quitting, you build a kind of durability that no pep talk can substitute for.

## Story

The email arrives on a Tuesday afternoon with the subject line you have been dreading: not this time, not you, try again next year. Your chest tightens and your first instinct is to shut the laptop, cancel the rest of the week, and tell yourself the whole thing was never meant to work out anyway. You have taken hits like this before, and more than once you stayed down longer than the pain actually required.

This time you let the sting run its course without making any permanent decisions. You take a long walk, eat something, sleep on it. The next morning you ask yourself a quieter question: what is still true? You are still capable. The goal still matters. One no is not the whole story of your career or your life.

You send a brief thank-you, ask what would have made the difference, and turn your attention to the parts you can actually control — the portfolio piece that was weak, the skill you have been postponing, the next application you almost talked yourself out of sending. None of it feels heroic. It feels like breathing after a body blow: ugly, necessary, and forward.

When the next opportunity comes, you are still standing and still working, not bitter in the stands explaining why the game is rigged. You took the hit. You did not quit. That is the muscle, and it compounds.

## Takeaway

Setbacks are not verdicts on your worth — they are tests of whether you will keep going after the sting fades. The people who eventually break through are rarely the ones who never get knocked down. They are the ones who absorb the punch, recover, and show up again before fear convinces them to stay on the ground.
