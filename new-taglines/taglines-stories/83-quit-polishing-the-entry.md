# Quit Polishing The Entry

*Quit Polishing The Entry*

The introduction is not the whole book — quit polishing the entry and march into the middle where the real work lives.

## Category

**Motivation** — Perfecting the opening keeps you from ever reaching the substance; *Quit Polishing The Entry* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by skipping the polished doorway and stepping into the messy middle.

## The idea

The introduction feels safe because it sets the tone, and setting the tone feels important. You rewrite the first paragraph, the first slide, the first email line, convinced that everything downstream depends on a flawless entry. The challenge is that the entry is not the work — it is the lobby. While you polish the lobby, the rooms that matter stay empty. When you quit polishing the entry and march into the middle, you discover what you are actually trying to say, and the opening often fixes itself once it has something real to introduce.

## Story

Lena had been drafting the opening chapter of her memoir for two months. She changed the first line fourteen times. Some versions started with childhood. Others opened on the day everything changed. Each version sounded literary for about an hour and wrong by the next morning.

Her editor sent a short note: write the scene you keep thinking about when you are in the shower. Stop trying to earn the reader at the door.

Lena skipped ahead to the kitchen argument she had been avoiding — the one where she finally said what she meant. The writing was rough and fast. She cried once, kept going, and finished six pages in an afternoon she had spent weeks preparing for.

When she went back to the beginning, the opening almost wrote itself. It needed to point at the kitchen, not replace it. She had been polishing the entry because the middle frightened her. The middle was where the book actually lived.

## Takeaway

If you have rewritten the beginning more than twice without moving forward, treat that as a signal. Jump to the part that scares or excites you and let the introduction follow the work instead of blocking it.
