# Let Done Lead

*Let Done Lead*

Let done lead and let perfect follow if it can — movement creates information that planning never will.

## Category

**Motivation** — Planning without delivery keeps you guessing; *Let Done Lead* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by finishing a rough version first and letting improvement follow real feedback instead of endless preparation.

## The idea

Planning feels safe because it stays private. You can refine forever without discovering whether the idea works, whether the structure holds, whether anyone needs what you are building. Done leads because movement creates information — what breaks, what lands, what should come next. Perfect, if it comes at all, follows from contact with reality, not from another pass over the outline. Let the finished rough version go first. Let refinement chase it instead of blocking it.

## Story

Claire had been developing a workshop for her team for six weeks. The slide deck was elegant. The timing was mapped to the minute. She had not run a single exercise with a real human because she wanted the flow to feel seamless before anyone saw it.

Her coworker Ben ran a messy pilot with half the material on a Wednesday afternoon. Slides out of order, one activity too long, another unclear. He came back with a list of fixes and a room full of people asking when the full version would run.

Claire felt exposed by the comparison until she recognized what Ben had bought with his rough run: data. He knew which section confused people. She knew only what looked good in isolation.

She scheduled a pilot for the following week with instructions she would normally consider unfinished. The opening stumbled. The middle worked better than she expected. Someone asked a question that made her rethink the whole closing segment. She adjusted that night with information no amount of solo planning had provided.

The final workshop was not perfect. It was good enough to help people, and good enough had started with letting done lead while she was still courting flawless in private.

## Takeaway

When you are stuck in preparation, ask what you would learn from a finished rough version that the outline cannot teach you. Ship the imperfect pass first. Let done lead, let perfect follow if it can, and trust movement to supply what planning has been pretending to know.
