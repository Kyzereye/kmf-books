# Ten Minutes Counts

*Ten Minutes Counts*

Ten minutes won't finish the project — it will prove you can enter it. Start with a sliver of time and let consistency do the heavy lifting.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for a large block of free time keeps projects theoretical; *Ten Minutes Counts* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by making a short, honest session enough to break the stall.

## The idea

The mind tells you that real work requires a cleared afternoon, a perfect setup, and the kind of uninterrupted focus that rarely appears in a normal week. So you postpone until conditions improve, and the project stays untouched because the entry fee feels too high. Ten minutes is small enough to slip past that gate. It will not finish the work, but it will prove you can enter it — and proof matters more than intention. Consistency built from slivers of time does what occasional heroic sessions cannot: it keeps the project alive between the days when you have more to give.

## Story

David wanted to learn Spanish before a trip in the spring. He downloaded an app, bought a workbook, and told himself he would study properly on weekends. Weekends filled up. March arrived with almost no practice beyond opening the app twice and closing it.

One Tuesday he had ten minutes before a meeting. He opened the app and did one lesson without negotiating for more time. The next day he did another ten minutes on the train. He did not feel like a serious student. He felt like a person who had touched the material two days in a row.

By the fourth week those slivers had become a habit he protected without thinking much about it. Some sessions stretched to twenty minutes because he was already inside the work. Others stayed at ten. When he landed in Barcelona he could order food, ask directions, and recover from mistakes without quitting the conversation.

David did not learn that fluency arrives in ten-minute bursts. He learned that ten minutes was enough to stop being someone who planned to study and become someone who actually did.

## Takeaway

When you are waiting for the perfect window, set a timer for ten minutes and enter the work on those terms alone. Small sessions repeated beat large intentions that never find a place on the calendar.
