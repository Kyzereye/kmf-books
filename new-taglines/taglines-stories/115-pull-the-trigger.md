# Pull The Trigger

*Pull The Trigger*

You have aimed long enough — pull the trigger on the decision you keep treating as theoretical.

## Category

**Motivation** — Endless deliberation disguises itself as wisdom; *Pull The Trigger* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by acting on a decision that has already been aimed at long enough to stop treating as hypothetical.

## The idea

Some decisions deserve patience. Others have simply been aimed at so long that more aiming has become a way to avoid the recoil. You have weighed the options, imagined the outcomes, and rehearsed the conversation in your head until the choice feels theoretical — something you are still studying rather than something you are living. At that point the missing piece is not more information. It is commitment. Pull the trigger on what you already know you need to do and let reality replace the simulation you have been living in.

## Story

James had been deciding whether to leave his firm for nearly a year. He had spreadsheets comparing compensation, long walks with his wife, late-night lists of pros and cons that always ended in the same place: he needed to go. He told himself he was being careful. He was being careful enough to stay comfortable.

A mentor asked a blunt question over coffee: if you already know what you would tell your son to do, why are you still researching? James had no good answer. He had aimed at the decision so thoroughly that pulling the trigger felt like the reckless part.

The next Monday he gave notice. His hands shook slightly when he sent the email. Nothing about the following weeks was smooth — awkward conversations, a slower search than he wanted, moments of doubt he had predicted and feared anyway. But the fog lifted in a way another month of deliberation never would have cleared.

He realized he had not been gathering information toward the end. He had been postponing the moment when the decision became real and therefore irreversible in the way that living always is. Aiming had protected him. Acting freed him.

## Takeaway

If you already know what you would advise someone else in your position, notice how long you have been exempting yourself from the same counsel. Some decisions are not waiting for more data. They are waiting for you to stop treating commitment as a future event and pull the trigger on what you have already aimed at long enough.
