# Begin Badly On Purpose

*Begin Badly On Purpose*

A rough start still counts — give yourself permission to be awkward, messy, and unfinished on the first pass. Beginning badly beats not beginning at all.

## Category

**Motivation** — The demand for a clean first attempt keeps you at zero; *Begin Badly On Purpose* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by lowering the bar until motion is allowed.

## The idea

The mind insists the first version must represent you, so you never produce a first version. The challenge is separating starting from performing. When you begin badly on purpose, you are not lowering your standards forever. You are refusing to let perfection block contact with the work. A bad first draft, a clumsy first rep, a halting first conversation — these are not failures. They are the entry fee for anything real.

## Story

Chris wanted to post educational videos but spent months researching microphones, lighting, and editing software. Every test recording looked wrong. He deleted twelve takes in one afternoon and told himself he would try again when he sounded natural.

His sister posted a shaky thirty-second clip from her phone about meal prep. It had bad audio and a typo in the caption. It also had comments from people who actually cooked the recipe.

Chris decided to begin badly on purpose. He recorded one take, left the small stumble in the middle, and published without watching it more than once. His face felt too close to the camera. The cut was rough. It was also live.

Three people messaged useful feedback. One person asked when the next video would come. The shame he expected did not arrive. What arrived was the strange relief of no longer being someone who only planned to start.

## Takeaway

If you are waiting to look good on the first try, you are waiting to never begin. Start badly on purpose once and you will learn how quickly the second try improves when the first one finally exists.
