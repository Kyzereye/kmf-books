# Chapters 71–80

## 71. Travel Deep Inside

*Go Inward First*

The most important journey you'll ever take doesn't require a passport — go inward, past the surface noise, and meet the person driving the car. Self-knowledge is the foundation every external win is built on.

## 72. Go to War

*War On Excuses*

Stop negotiating with your excuses and declare war on the habits, fears, and distractions standing between you and what you want. Fight for your life with the seriousness it deserves.

## 73. Use the 5 Percent Solution

*Five Percent More Daily*

You don't need a revolution — you need five percent more effort, five percent more focus, five percent more consistency than yesterday. Small edges compounded daily will bury your old limitations.

## 74. Do Something Badly

*Do It Badly Now*

Perfection is the enemy of progress — give yourself permission to do it badly, do it messy, and do it now. A rough draft beats a blank page every single time.

## 75. Learn Visioneering

*See It Then Build It*

See the future so clearly in your mind that you can reverse-engineer the steps to get there. Vision without a plan is a daydream; vision with engineering behind it is a blueprint.

## 76. Lighten Things Up

*Lighten The Hell Up*

Take your goals seriously but don't take yourself so seriously that you grind the joy out of the pursuit. A lighter spirit moves faster, lasts longer, and attracts better company.

## 77. Serve and Grow Rich

*Serve Then Grow Rich*

The fastest way to build something meaningful is to focus on how you can serve others — value given freely comes back multiplied. Grow rich by making other people's lives better.

## 78. Make a List of Your Life

*Name What You Want*

Write down everything you want — not someday, but in this life — and let the list shock you into honesty about what you're actually working toward. You can't hit a target you haven't named.

## 79. Set a Specific Power Goal

*One Goal All Power*

Vague goals produce vague results — pick one specific target that excites and scares you, and pour your energy into it. A power goal is a magnet that organizes everything else around it.

## 80. Change Yourself First

*Change You First*

Stop trying to fix the world, your boss, or your circumstances before you fix your own thinking and habits. The only thing you truly control is you — start there and watch everything else shift.
