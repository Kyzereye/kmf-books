# Finish The First Inch

*Finish The First Inch*

You do not need the whole mile — finish the first inch completely and let completion teach you how to continue.

## Category

**Motivation** — The size of the whole project blocks the first move; *Finish The First Inch* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by completing one tiny segment fully instead of vaguely chipping at the whole.

## The idea

You stare at the full distance — the book, the renovation, the course, the career change — and every small effort feels insultingly incomplete. The challenge is that vague partial effort does not teach you much. It keeps you in the anxiety of the whole without the confidence of anything finished. When you finish the first inch completely — one drawer organized, one section drafted, one lesson recorded — completion itself becomes the teacher. You learn how the work feels, what comes next, and that finished pieces stack in a way that endless starting never does.

## Story

Greg bought supplies to repaint his home office and then stared at the walls for a week. The room was not large, but "repaint the office" lived in his head as a weekend-sized monster that required perfect weather, perfect energy, and no other obligations.

His neighbor stopped by and noticed the tape still in the bag. "Do one wall," he said. "Not the room. One wall, done right."

Greg chose the shortest wall. He moved the furniture, taped the trim, cut in the edges, rolled the paint, and cleaned the brushes that night. One wall. Fully finished. Not "started the project."

Standing in front of a completed surface changed something. He could see the color. He could see the next edge. The mile had not shrunk in reality, but the first inch was real, and real completion showed him the second inch was just more of the same kind of work. By Sunday the room was done. It had started when he stopped treating a wall like a fraction too small to count.

## Takeaway

Choose one inch of the project and finish it all the way, not halfway. Let the feeling of done on a small piece show you that the mile is built from inches you can actually complete.
