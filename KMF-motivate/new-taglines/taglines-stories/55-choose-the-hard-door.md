# Choose The Hard Door

*Choose The Hard Door*

Two doors — one easy, one right. The easy door keeps you comfortable and stuck; walk through the hard one and the day changes shape.

## Category

**Motivation** — Comfort whispers reasons to delay; *Choose The Hard Door* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by deliberately entering the difficult option first instead of waiting for courage to arrive.

## The idea

Every day offers a soft exit: postpone, simplify, defer until conditions improve. The hard door is the one you can already see — the conversation, the application, the first workout, the honest draft. Waiting to want it is another way of choosing the easy door by default. Courage is not a feeling you collect first. It is what you practice by opening the harder handle while your hands still hesitate.

## Story

Priya had two choices after the client call went sideways. The easy door was to send a vague follow-up, smooth the tone, and hope the problem aged into silence. The hard door was to call back that afternoon, admit where the delivery missed, and propose a fix that would cost her team a weekend.

She wanted to sleep on it. She recognized the pattern: sleep on it often meant never quite returning to the heat.

She chose the hard door. The call was uncomfortable for four minutes. The client was frustrated, then specific, then willing to stay. The weekend was real, but so was the trust she would have lost in the easy version.

Nothing about the choice felt heroic in the moment. It felt early and exposed. Later she noticed the difference in her own posture: she had stopped waiting to feel brave and started practicing bravery as a decision she could make before lunch.

## Takeaway

You rarely need more time. You need to name the hard door you are avoiding and walk through it while you still can. The easy door will always be there. It will also always cost you something quieter than failure — momentum.
