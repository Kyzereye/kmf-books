# Rise Before Excuses

*Rise Before Excuses*

Your excuses wake up when you do — beat them out of bed and get moving before they finish their first sentence. Win the first hour and excuses lose their grip on the rest.

## Category

**Discipline** — Excuses win when they get the first word; *Rise Before Excuses* answers "How do I show up even when I don't feel like it?" by acting before the negotiation in your head fully wakes up.

## The idea

The moment you wake up, a familiar negotiation begins — too tired, too cold, tomorrow will be better — and if you lie still long enough, the excuses finish their argument before your feet ever hit the floor. When you move first, even clumsily, you take the microphone away from the voice that would gladly run your whole day. Win the first hour and the rest of the schedule has less room for retreat.

## Story

The alarm went off at five-thirty and your first conscious thought was already a rebuttal. The room was cold. You had slept badly. You could go tonight instead, or tomorrow when you felt more rested, or next week when life calmed down. You had lost this negotiation a hundred times, and you knew exactly how the day would end — rushed, guilty, another promise moved to the evening that would never come.

That night you placed the alarm across the room and laid out your shoes by the door. When it rang, you stood up before the story could build its case. Water. Shoes. Out the door — not a perfect workout, just motion while the debate was still groggy.

The run was short and your breathing was ugly, but by the time you showered and sat down for breakfast, the hardest fight of the day was already over. The excuse that would have owned your afternoon never got its opening. You ate knowing you had won something real before most people had finished their first cup of coffee.

It became a pattern you could feel in your bones. The first hour belonged to action, not argument. The excuses did not vanish forever — they just arrived too late to steer the whole day. You had learned to rise before they finished their first sentence, and that small victory at dawn changed the tone of everything that followed.

## Takeaway

The morning is where most days are decided, often before you notice a decision was being made. Moving before the negotiation fully wakes up is one of the cheapest wins available to you.
