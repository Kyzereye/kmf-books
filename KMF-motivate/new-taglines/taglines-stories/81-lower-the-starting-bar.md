# Lower The Starting Bar

*Lower The Starting Bar*

If you cannot start high, start low — lower the bar until movement is possible and raise it after you are already in motion.

## Category

**Motivation** — An impossibly high entry point keeps you frozen at zero; *Lower The Starting Bar* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by shrinking the first step until your body can actually take it.

## The idea

The mind sets the opening move at the level of the finished product, then wonders why you never begin. A novel becomes "write chapter one," chapter one becomes "write something brilliant," and brilliant becomes a reason to stay on the couch. The challenge is not ambition but the height of the first requirement. When you lower the starting bar until movement is possible — five minutes, one sentence, one lap — you trade shame for contact with the work. Once you are in motion, you can raise the bar from inside the effort instead of negotiating with it from the outside.

## Story

Rachel had promised herself she would get back to running after a long winter indoors. Every morning she laced her shoes, looked at the hill behind her house, and went back inside to make coffee. Three miles felt like the minimum. Three miles kept winning.

Her physical therapist had said something simple on her last visit: start with what you can do, not what you used to do. Rachel laughed it off. That afternoon she put her shoes on again and walked to the end of the driveway.

The next day she walked to the mailbox. The day after that she jogged thirty seconds and walked the rest. It felt embarrassingly small. It also felt like something.

By the second week she was running half a mile without stopping. The hill was still there. She was no longer arguing with it from the kitchen. She had lowered the bar until her feet could move, and motion did what motivation had refused to do — it showed up after she started.

## Takeaway

When you cannot begin, ask whether the first step is sized for a person in motion or a person on the couch. Lower the bar until movement is possible, then let standards rise from inside the work instead of blocking the door.
