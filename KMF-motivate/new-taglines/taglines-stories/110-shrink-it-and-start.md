# Shrink It And Start

*Shrink It And Start*

Make the task so small it cannot argue back — shrink it and start before your brain rebuilds the wall.

## Category

**Motivation** — Large tasks trigger resistance that feels like reason; *Shrink It And Start* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by reducing the first move until it is too small to debate and too fast to postpone.

## The idea

The brain is skilled at inflating work until it deserves another day. A report becomes a reputation decision. A phone call becomes a confrontation. A workout becomes a lifestyle overhaul. The challenge is not motivation in the abstract — it is the size of the entry point. When you shrink the task, you remove the material your resistance uses to build its case. You are not abandoning ambition. You are sneaking past the gatekeeper with something too minor to trigger a full negotiation.

## Story

Walter needed to clean out his garage before his daughter's graduation party. He stood in the driveway every weekend, looked at the packed shelves, and calculated how many hours the job would take. The calculation always ended the same way — not today.

His neighbor watched him retreat to the kitchen twice in one morning. "What's the smallest thing you could do in five minutes?" she asked. Walter said he could probably clear one shelf by the door.

He set a timer and did only that. One shelf. Boxes down, sweep, done. The garage still looked crowded, but one visible section had changed. The wall in his mind thinned.

The next day he cleared the floor space in front of that shelf. Then a corner. Within two weeks the garage was party-ready, built from five-minute starts he could not argue with. The whole job had been sitting behind a first step his brain kept resizing upward every time he approached it.

Walter learned that resistance was not about the garage. It was about the size of the opening he offered himself.

## Takeaway

When a task feels immovable, cut the first step until it takes less than five minutes and cannot reasonably be refused. Start that version immediately, before your mind rebuilds the wall around the larger job.
