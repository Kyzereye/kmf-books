# Do The Next Small Thing

*Do The Next Small Thing*

When the whole project feels too big to touch, shrink it until your hands can reach it — one email, one page, one rep. Motion starts when the step is small enough to do.

## Category

**Motivation** — Overwhelm freezes you at the size of the whole; *Do The Next Small Thing* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by making the first action small enough that starting is no longer a debate.

## The idea

The mind refuses to begin when the task is framed as everything at once. You are not failing because you lack ambition; you are stalled because the entry point is invisible. When you shrink the work to the next physical action — open the file, write one paragraph, make one call — you give yourself something you can control right now. Small starts are not small lives. They are how large lives actually get built.

## Story

The garage renovation lived in your head as a single impossible object: permits, budget, tools, weekends you did not have. Every time you thought about starting, you saw the whole mountain and closed the door.

One Saturday you changed the question. Not "How do I finish this?" but "What is the next small thing?" You swept one corner. You measured one wall. You watched one tutorial about drywall repair and stopped after one tutorial.

None of it felt like progress in the dramatic sense. By the third weekend you had cleared a workspace. By the second month you had framed one section. The project was still large, but it was no longer theoretical. You had learned something important: you did not need to feel ready for the entire renovation. You only needed to be willing to do the next piece your hands could actually hold.

The garage is not done. It is moving. That difference began when you stopped asking the whole project to fit in one afternoon.

## Takeaway

When you are stuck, the problem is often the size of the frame, not the size of your ability. Find the next action so small it feels almost silly, do it, and let momentum learn your name from there.
