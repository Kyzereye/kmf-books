# Put Skin In Game

*Put Skin In Game*

When something real is on the line — your money, your reputation, your word — you show up differently. Put skin in the game and watch how fast your seriousness catches up to your ambition.

## Category

**Ownership** — Low stakes make low effort feel acceptable; *Put Skin In Game* answers "How do I take ownership of my results?" by putting something real at risk so seriousness matches ambition.

## The idea

Dreams stay hypothetical when walking away costs nothing — free plans, vague launch dates, ideas you can abandon without a receipt. Put something real on the line: money you won't get back, a public deadline, a reputation you've staked on your word. When the cost of quitting is tangible, your behavior changes before your feelings catch up, and seriousness finally matches ambition.

## Story

You've talked about writing a newsletter for eighteen months. The domain is registered. The template is saved. You haven't sent issue one, because failure is still free — no audience to disappoint, no invoice to explain, no story to untell.

You announce a launch date to two hundred people and pay for a year of the platform upfront — nonrefundable. You tell three friends you'll buy dinner if you miss the date. Something shifts immediately. Outlines you'd been "thinking about" become drafts with deadlines. You record a voice memo intro, hate it, record again. Launch morning your hands shake hitting send. Twelve people open it. Two reply. That's not fame. That's proof the stake was real.

Issue two arrives faster than issue one, not because writing got easier, but because quitting now would mean admitting the commitment was theater. By issue ten you've found a rhythm that free intention never produced. The audience is still small. Your follow-through isn't.

Skin in the game didn't guarantee a bestseller. It guaranteed you wouldn't treat the dream like a hobby you could shelf without consequence — and that guarantee turned out to be the whole difference.

## Takeaway

Ambition without stakes is mostly storytelling — you can want something sincerely and still behave like it doesn't matter because nothing painful happens when you stop. Skin in the game converts intention into behavior by attaching a cost to retreat, and that cost is often what your discipline was missing. The goal isn't reckless betting; it's making the gap between what you say and what you do uncomfortable enough to close.
