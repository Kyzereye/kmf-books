# Move Before Ready

*Move Before Ready*

Ready is a feeling that shows up after you start, not before — move anyway and let readiness catch up to action. Quit waiting for the green light from your emotions; beginning badly beats preparing forever.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for the right feeling is another form of not starting; *Move Before Ready* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by acting before readiness arrives and letting the feeling catch up to motion.

## The idea

The thing that matters will rarely arrive with full preparation attached. Waiting for emotional certainty before you begin is often fear wearing polite clothes, and the people who move ahead are not more capable on day one. They simply started before the feeling cooperated. When you move with what you have, awkwardness and doubt do not disappear, but they stop being the gatekeeper. Readiness tends to show up in the middle, after you have already been imperfect in public.

## Story

Nadia had been offered a chance to lead a community workshop on a topic she knew well. She asked for two weeks to prepare, then two more. She built slides, reorganized slides, and watched videos about public speaking, but she could not shake the sense that she needed one more day to feel legitimate at the front of the room.

The organizer set a date three days out. Nadia considered declining. She had told herself she would begin when the timing felt right, waiting each morning for a burst of clarity that never came. Meanwhile a colleague with half her preparation joined a similar program, struggled through the first sessions, and was already months ahead by summer.

Nadia accepted the date, trimmed her material to what she could deliver without a script, and showed up with notes in her hand and nerves she could not hide. She lost her place once and laughed about it. People stayed anyway. They asked questions. One attendee said the rough honesty made the material easier to trust.

On the drive home she felt something she had been trying to manufacture before starting: readiness. It had not come first. It had caught up. The people still waiting for the perfect moment were still waiting. She had moved before ready, and moving was what changed everything.

## Takeaway

Treat unreadiness as a companion, not a stop sign. Most meaningful steps feel slightly too early from the inside because growth asks you to operate before you have proof. Move with what you have, learn in motion, and let competence meet you on the path instead of at the door.
