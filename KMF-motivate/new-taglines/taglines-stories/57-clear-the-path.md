# Clear The Path

*Clear The Path*

You cannot start what you cannot see — remove one obstacle, one distraction, one clutter pile, and the next step finally has room to land.

## Category

**Motivation** — Hidden friction blocks the first move; *Clear The Path* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by making the starting line visible before asking for more willpower.

## The idea

Sometimes you are not lazy. You are buried. The desk is chaos, the tab count is absurd, the task is named so vaguely that your brain cannot find the handle. Willpower is a weak tool against a muddy starting line. When you clear one layer of obstruction — close the wrong tabs, name the actual next step, put the tools in reach — starting stops feeling like a personality test and starts feeling like a physical motion you can perform.

## Story

Lena sat down to work on her side business every evening for two weeks and somehow spent the hour organizing nothing important. The problem was not desire. The problem was that "work on the business" was not a task. It was a cloud.

One night she tried a different move. She closed every tab except the invoice template. She put her notebook open to a single question: "What is the next sale I can make?" She moved the laundry basket off the chair. The whole setup took seven minutes.

Then she made one call she had been avoiding. Just one. The path had been there, but it had been covered in vague intention.

She did not become a different person. She became a person who could see the first step. Clearing the path, she learned, was not procrastination dressed up as preparation. It was the honest work of making start possible.

## Takeaway

Before you blame motivation, look at the starting line. Remove one piece of friction, name one concrete action, and see whether the problem was desire or visibility.
