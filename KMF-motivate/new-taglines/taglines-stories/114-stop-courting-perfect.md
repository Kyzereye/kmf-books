# Stop Courting Perfect

*Stop Courting Perfect*

Perfect is a flirtation that never commits — stop courting it and start something real that can actually grow.

## Category

**Motivation** — Perfectionism promises safety but never delivers a start; *Stop Courting Perfect* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by ending the endless refinement cycle and beginning with something imperfect that can improve through contact with reality.

## The idea

Perfect is seductive because it never asks you to be seen. You can stay in preparation forever, adjusting, researching, refining, telling yourself you are being thorough when you are actually avoiding exposure. It flirts with your standards and never commits to a date. The shift is to stop courting what will not arrive and start something real — rough, flawed, and alive enough to grow. Real work collects feedback. Perfect work collects drafts. Only one of those can change your life.

## Story

Lena had been designing a portfolio for eight months. Every version was closer to the version in her head, which moved every time she approached it. She chose new fonts on Sundays. She rebuilt the case studies twice. Friends asked for the link. She said she wanted it right first.

Her former classmate Marco published a portfolio with three projects, uneven photos, and a contact form that occasionally broke. Within a month he had two freelance inquiries. Lena told herself his standards were lower. She also noticed he was working and she was curating.

One afternoon she closed the mood board and opened a plain template. She uploaded four projects with captions she would normally rewrite twelve times. She hit publish before she could talk herself out of it, then walked away from the screen.

The site was not the portfolio she had imagined. It was a portfolio. A designer she admired replied with a note about one case study and a typo in the footer. Lena fixed the typo that night and felt something she had not felt in months: forward motion. Perfect had kept her elegant and invisible. Real had made her findable.

## Takeaway

Notice where refinement has become a way to avoid being evaluated. Perfect will always suggest one more pass. Start something real instead — imperfect, public, and capable of growing through contact with the world rather than contact with your fear of not being enough.
