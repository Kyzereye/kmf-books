# Ship At Seventy Percent

*Ship At Seventy Percent*

Seventy percent ready and shipped beats one hundred percent stuck in your head. Send the version you have and improve from contact with reality.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for full readiness blocks the first delivery; *Ship At Seventy Percent* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by releasing a real version now instead of protecting a perfect version that never leaves you.

## The idea

The last thirty percent of polish often costs ninety percent of the delay. You tell yourself you are being careful when you are actually avoiding the moment someone can respond to your work. Shipping at seventy percent is not sloppiness for its own sake. It is the decision to learn in public, to let feedback arrive, and to improve from something that exists instead of something you endlessly refine in private.

## Story

Aisha's team had been "almost ready" to launch an internal tool for six weeks. Features debated, edge cases catalogued, one more review always scheduled. Meanwhile coworkers kept using the broken workaround because the perfect tool lived only in development.

She proposed a rule: ship at seventy percent on Friday, fix on Monday. Leadership winced. She shipped anyway — documentation thin, two buttons labeled wrong, core function working.

Monday the inbox filled with useful complaints. Real users found the problems her team had been imagining in the abstract. By Wednesday the tool was better than any of the pre-launch drafts because improvement had a target.

The product did not succeed because it was unfinished. It succeeded because being unfinished in the wild beat being immaculate in a room nobody entered.

## Takeaway

If you are stuck at ninety-nine percent, ask what you are protecting besides your pride. Ship at seventy, learn from what comes back, and let reality finish what fear kept delaying.
