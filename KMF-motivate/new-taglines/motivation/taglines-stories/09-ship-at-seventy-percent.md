# Ship At Seventy Percent

*Ship At Seventy Percent*

Seventy percent ready and shipped beats one hundred percent stuck in your head. Send the version you have and improve from contact with reality.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for full readiness blocks the first delivery; *Ship At Seventy Percent* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by releasing a real version now instead of protecting a perfect version that never leaves you.


## Theme

**Start Moving** — Releasing at seventy percent is the move from stuck-in-head perfection to contact with reality.
## The idea

The last thirty percent of polish often costs ninety percent of the delay. You tell yourself you are being careful when you are actually avoiding the moment someone can respond to your work. Shipping at seventy percent is not sloppiness for its own sake. It is the decision to learn in public, to let feedback arrive, and to improve from something that exists instead of something you endlessly refine in private.

## Story
<!-- story shape: Failure / near-miss -->

Two siblings had the same idea for a small app that would solve a problem at their company, and both built prototypes while knowing the launch window was narrow, one quarter before a competitor's announcement would close the gap. One kept polishing, cataloguing edge cases, rewriting documentation, relabeling buttons for the fourth time, and "almost ready" stretched into six weeks and then eight. By the time the prototype reached ninety-eight percent the window had passed, the competitor shipped first, and coworkers kept using the broken workaround because the perfect tool lived only on one person's laptop.

The sibling shipped at seventy percent on a Friday with thin documentation and two buttons labeled wrong, but the core function worked. Monday brought useful complaints from real users, and by Wednesday the app had been improved three times based on contact with people who actually needed it. The cost of waiting was not abstract: one version never left the room, and the other learned in public and kept moving.

## Takeaway

If you are stuck at ninety-nine percent, ask what you are protecting besides your pride — and what you are losing while you wait. Ship at seventy, learn from what comes back, and let reality finish what fear kept delaying.
