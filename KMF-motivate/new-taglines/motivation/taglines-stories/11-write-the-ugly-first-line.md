# Write The Ugly First Line

*Write The Ugly First Line*

The first line does not have to sing — it has to exist. Write the ugly opening and give the rest of the page something to follow.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for a perfect opening keeps the page blank indefinitely; *Write The Ugly First Line* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by putting any first sentence on the page so the rest of the work has something to build from.


## Theme

**Start Moving** — Writing an ugly opening that exists on the page gives momentum something to follow — a pure start-moving move.
## The idea

A blank page feels demanding because the first line seems to set the standard for everything after it. You hunt for something clever, true, and strong enough to justify the project — and the hunt becomes the project. The ugly first line breaks that trap. It does not announce your talent. It announces that writing has begun. Once something is on the page, you have material to revise, reject, or follow. The rest of the piece rarely appears until the first line stops being theoretical.

## Story
<!-- story shape: Question structure -->

The annual report chapter was due Friday, and for two mornings the cursor had blinked while she tested elegant openings in her head and rejected each one before it touched the screen. She kept asking whether the opening had to carry the whole year in one sentence, whether the first line was too plain and the board would read indifference into it, whether a clever hook would sound like marketing and an honest hook would sound like failure, and whether one more hour might bring the right phrase when the right phrase was exactly what kept the page blank.

Finally she asked herself a different question: what is the ugliest true sentence I can put down without lying? She typed, "This year was harder than we expected." It sat there unadorned, almost rude in its simplicity, and she did not watch it for approval. She wrote the next sentence about a launch that slipped, then a paragraph about a team that stayed late through a rough quarter, and the ugly line did not sing. It held the door open.

## Takeaway

When the page stays blank because the opening is not good enough yet, type the plainest true sentence you can and keep going. You are not writing the final first line. You are writing the one that makes the second line possible.
