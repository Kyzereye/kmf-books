# Make The Call First

*Make The Call First*

The call you're avoiding is usually the logjam. Make it first and watch the rest of your list get lighter.

## Category

**Motivation** — One dreaded task blocks everything behind it; *Make The Call First* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by clearing the avoided conversation before smaller tasks can pretend to count as progress.


## Theme

**Push Through** — The avoided call is the logjam — making it first pushes through the blockage holding everything else back.
## The idea

Not all tasks weigh the same. One phone call, one email, one honest conversation sits at the center of your list and quietly prevents everything else from feeling finished. You answer easy messages, tidy your desk, and organize subtasks while the real blockage stays untouched. When you make the call first — before the comfortable work, before the excuses warm up — you remove the logjam. The rest of the list stops feeling like a wall and starts behaving like a sequence again.

## Story

Rachel's Monday list had twelve items, but only one of them mattered. She needed to call a client and admit the timeline had slipped. She had drafted the conversation in her head a dozen times and imagined every version of his disappointment. Instead of calling, she reorganized her inbox, updated a spreadsheet, and answered three messages that could have waited.

At ten o'clock her manager asked for a status update. Rachel realized she had been busy all morning without moving the project forward. The unmade call was the reason nothing else felt quite done.

She stepped outside with her phone. She dialed before she could negotiate. The client answered on the second ring. She told him the truth in four sentences. He was frustrated, but he was not surprised. They agreed on a revised date and one next step for that afternoon.

When Rachel returned to her desk, the other eleven items looked different. Two of them took less than ten minutes each. One disappeared entirely because the call had resolved it. She had not become someone who loves hard conversations. She had learned that the call she avoided was holding the rest of her day hostage.

## Takeaway

Scan your list for the one item you are circling instead of touching. Do that first, even badly, and notice how much of the remaining work was waiting on permission you already had.
