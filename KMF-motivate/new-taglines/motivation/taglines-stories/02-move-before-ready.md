# Move Before Ready

*Move Before Ready*

Ready is a feeling that shows up after you start, not before — move anyway and let readiness catch up to action. Quit waiting for the green light from your emotions; beginning badly beats preparing forever.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting for the right feeling is another form of not starting; *Move Before Ready* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by acting before readiness arrives and letting the feeling catch up to motion.


## Theme

**Start Moving** — This entry belongs here because it treats beginning as the trigger for readiness, not the other way around — action first, feelings follow.
## The idea

The thing that matters will rarely arrive with full preparation attached. Waiting for emotional certainty before you begin is often fear wearing polite clothes, and the people who move ahead are not more capable on day one. They simply started before the feeling cooperated. When you move with what you have, awkwardness and doubt do not disappear, but they stop being the gatekeeper. Readiness tends to show up in the middle, after you have already been imperfect in public.

## Story
<!-- story shape: In medias res -->

Nadia stood at the front of the room with notes in her hand that had begun to shake. Twelve chairs faced a projector that hummed too loudly, and she had asked for two more weeks before accepting a date three days out because the organizer would not move it again. Her slides existed, but she had trimmed them last night when a full script started to feel like something she could hide behind.

That morning she watched a video about posture, reorganized her bag twice, and almost sent a message declining. She did not send it. She lost her place on the second point and laughed at herself, and people stayed anyway. They asked questions she had never rehearsed, and one person said the rough honesty made the material easier to trust.

On the drive home she felt something she had been trying to manufacture before she started: readiness. It had not arrived at the door. It caught up somewhere in the middle, after she was already standing there, imperfect and in motion.

## Takeaway

Treat unreadiness as a companion, not a stop sign. Most meaningful steps feel slightly too early from the inside because growth asks you to operate before you have proof. Move with what you have, learn in motion, and let competence meet you on the path instead of at the door.
