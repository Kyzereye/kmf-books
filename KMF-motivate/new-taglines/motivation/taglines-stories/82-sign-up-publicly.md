# Sign Up Publicly

*Sign Up Publicly*

Registration is a start. Sign up publicly — the class, the race, the deadline — and let the commitment pull you forward when feeling will not.

## Category

**Motivation** — Waiting to feel ready lets you postpone indefinitely; *Sign Up Publicly* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by locking in a date and a public record before motivation shows up.

## Theme

**Commit & Own It** — Public registration puts your name on a calendar the world can see, so the commitment pulls you forward when feeling alone will not.

## The idea

You can intend to take the class, run the race, or meet the deadline for years while never crossing the line from thinking to enrolled. Registration feels like paperwork, but it is actually a decision with edges. Once your name is on the list, time moves toward you instead of away from you. There is a date. There is a receipt. There are people who expect the room to contain you. Feeling may not arrive before the start. The signup can. Let the commitment do the pulling when your mood refuses to push.

## Story

Marcus told himself he would get back in shape when travel slowed down. Travel never slowed down. He walked past the community pool's bulletin board every Thursday and read the masters swim schedule like it was someone else's life.

One morning a lane mate from years ago called him out. "You still talking about coming back or you signing up?" Marcus deflected with a joke. The friend sent him the registration link in a text before he could change the subject.

Marcus opened it at his desk, between two meetings, with no heroic speech prepared. He entered his name, paid the fee, and screenshotted the confirmation before his brain could reopen negotiations. He posted nothing grand — just sent the screenshot to his friend with "Tuesday."

The first week he did not want to go. His suit felt tight. He was slower than he remembered. But he had signed publicly in the only sense that mattered: his name was on the roster, and the coach greeted him like a person who belonged to the season, not a person who might show up someday.

By March he was not motivated every morning. He was registered every Tuesday and Thursday, and that difference carried him through the days feeling would have skipped.

## Takeaway

Find the class, race, cohort, or deadline you keep circling in your head and register before you feel qualified. Public signup is not performative bravery — it is a practical way to borrow momentum from a date that will arrive whether you feel ready or not. Let the commitment on the calendar do what waiting for inspiration never will.
