# Push Through The Threshold

*Push Through The Threshold*

Starting has a threshold — the first uncomfortable minute — push through it and the work often gets easier on the other side.

## Category

**Motivation** — The initial resistance feels like a verdict; *Push Through The Threshold* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by treating the first uncomfortable minute as a gate to pass through, not a signal to stop.


## Theme

**Push Through** — The first uncomfortable minute is the threshold — pushing through it is the core push-through move.
## The idea

Every worthwhile start has a threshold: the first minute when your body wants to quit, your mind offers a dozen reasons to delay, and the task feels heavier than it will five minutes from now. Most people treat that discomfort as proof they are not ready. The shift is simpler — push through the threshold once and let the work teach you whether resistance was wisdom or habit. Often the other side is not easy, but it is workable, and workable is enough to keep going.

## Story

Elena sat at her desk with a blank document open and a training run on her calendar. She had been circling both all morning. The run felt cold outside. The document felt like it might expose how little she knew. She refreshed her email twice and told herself she would start after lunch.

At one o'clock she put on her shoes without negotiating. The first three minutes were exactly as bad as she expected — tight legs, shallow breath, the urge to turn back toward the warm house. She did not argue with the feeling. She counted to sixty twice and kept her feet moving.

By minute eight her stride found a rhythm. By minute twenty she was glad she had gone. The run did not become effortless. It became possible, and possible was the version she had been avoiding.

That afternoon she applied the same rule to the document. She wrote one rough paragraph while still uncomfortable, then another. The threshold was not a wall. It was a door she had been standing in front of, treating the draft of cold air as a reason never to step through.

## Takeaway

When resistance spikes at the beginning, ask whether you have actually started or only approached starting. Give the first uncomfortable minute a fair trial before you let it speak for the whole task. Most of the time the work on the other side is not easier — but you are already in it, and that changes everything.
