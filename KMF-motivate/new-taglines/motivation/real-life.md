## How to use

1. Capture a memory using the **entry template** below (rough bullets are enough).
2. Tag it with a **life domain** and one or more **book chapters**.
3. When a tagline story is ready to write, pull from this bank and match to book # + story shape.
4. One strong memory can serve multiple taglines from different angles.

### USS Charles F. Adams — satellite surveillance brief

- **Year / age (rough):** 1987, U.S. Navy
- **Life domain:** Work / career (military)
- **Chapter(s):** Start Moving
- **Book #:** 02 — Move Before Ready
- **What I was avoiding:** Giving the brief before I felt prepared — first asked for half an hour, then tried to buy time with notes
- **What I actually did:** Agreed to five minutes to jot notes and gave the ten-minute report anyway, nervous, to a bigger audience than expected
- **What happened next:** CO gave an atta-boy for the presentation despite sweating, voice breaking, and not being ready in the way I wanted
- **Physical detail:** Normal night watch, nothing going on; Senior Chief poked his head into the EX shack; started report as CO and XO stepped in for end-of-day rounds
- **Who else was involved:** Senior Chief (boss), fellow sailors (expected audience), CO, XO
- **Story type:** In medias res / before ready — moved before prep felt sufficient
- **Source:** mine
- **Status:** seed
- **Notes:** One of my duties was tracking which satellites were overhead and what they could "see"; command officers decided what to do with the information. Brief covered what satellite surveillance was, types of surveillance, and what could be done if anything. Was shy and bad at public speaking even among sailors. Readiness caught up after I was already in it — maps to story shape in [02-move-before-ready.md](taglines-stories/02-move-before-ready.md).

**Raw (as told):**

> It was 1987, and I was in the US Navy onboard the Charles F. Adams — DDG-2. It was a normal night watch with nothing going on when my Senior Chief poked his head inside the EX shack and asked me to come out and explain satellite surveillance. As one of my duties, I was responsible for knowing which satellites were overhead, and what they could "see." It was up to the command officers to decide what they would do with the information. I was asked to give a 10 minute brief about what satellite surveillance was, the types of surveillance, and what could be done if anything. I told my boss to give me 1/2 an hour to put something together. I could see by the expression on his face that he was disappointed. I then said to give me 5 min ish to jot down some notes. I was pretty shy then, and was really bad at public speaking even though the public was just a bunch of sailors. I was expecting just a bunch of my fellow sailors to be the audience, but as I started my report, the CO and the XO stepped in for their end of the day rounds. I was dripping sweat I was so nervous, and I am sure my voice broke many times, but I gave the report and got an atta-boy from the CO for my presentation.

### Starting the consulting business — one step at a time

- **Year / age (rough):** Years of wanting it; stepped in when ready to leave W2 safety
- **Life domain:** Work / career (entrepreneurship)
- **Chapter(s):** Start Moving
- **Book #:** 04 — Do The Next Small Thing
- **What I was avoiding:** Launching the whole business at once — name, LLC, taxes, clients, membership projects all figured out before moving
- **What I actually did:** Read books, watched videos, asked friends who already had companies; got first client before company name was set; then LLC, business bank account, taxes; second short-term client; kept learning and applying each piece
- **What happened next:** More clients seemed to appear; stopped applying for W2 jobs and talking to recruiters; still scary, still unsure what's next, but moving forward
- **Physical detail:** —
- **Who else was involved:** Friends with their own companies; first clients
- **Story type:** Chronicle — small steps compounding; each piece built on the last
- **Source:** mine
- **Status:** seed
- **Notes:** Did well writing code at jobs but never fully satisfied; thought own thing would feel better. W2 = safe (paycheck, benefits); own business = unknown. Vision: software consulting (clients) or membership-driven projects. Didn't know how to run a business — learned as I went. Built it one thing then the next, not all at once. Maps to story shape in [04-do-the-next-small-thing.md](taglines-stories/04-do-the-next-small-thing.md).

**Raw (as told):**

> I had been wanting to start my own business for years. I did well in the few places I wrote code, but I was always not satisfied with something. Not sure what it was, but I thought doing my own thing would make me feel better. But a good job with a good steady paycheck and benefits was safe and secure, and my own business was the unknown. I know what I wanted to do — software consulting, either get clients or do my own membership driven projects, but I didn't know anything about running my own business. So I read some books and watched some videos and asked some friends who already had their own companies questions. I got my first client before I even had my company name hammered down. Then I got a LLC, and business bank account. Started learning about taxes. Before I knew it I was moving forward and I got another client with a short term project. I read some more books and watched some more videos using that information to move myself forward. And then more people seemed to just pop up with need of my services. It still is a bit scary and I am still not sure what will be next, but I am no longer applying for W2 jobs or talking with recruiters. I built the business not by building it all at once, but doing one thing and then the next, learning some more and then putting that into practice each piece building on the last.
