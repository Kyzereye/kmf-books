# Cut The Excuse

*Cut The Excuse*

The moment you catch yourself negotiating with your own weakness, cut it — no rebuttal, no second hearing, just done. Excuses survive on attention; starve them.

## Category

**Mindset** — Negotiating with weakness gives excuses oxygen; *Cut The Excuse* answers "How do I change the way I think about this?" by ending the inner debate the moment you catch it starting.

## The idea

Your excuses arrive on schedule, polished and persuasive, ready to negotiate you back into comfort. The moment you catch yourself entertaining them, you have a choice: run a full trial in your head or cut the conversation short. Excuses survive on attention, and when you refuse to give them a second hearing, you are left with the only question that actually moves you forward — what are you going to do now?

## Story

Your alarm goes off at 5:30 and before your feet hit the floor the excuses are already lined up: too cold, too tired, you will go tonight instead, one rest day will not matter in the grand scheme of things. You used to debate them. You would lie there for twenty minutes running a courtroom in your head — prosecution, defense, hung jury — and then accidentally sleep until seven. The run never happened at night either. It never does.

This morning you notice the pattern mid-sentence. "I'm too—" You cut it off. Not angry, not pumped up, just closed. Shoes on, door out, no further argument scheduled. The run is short and ugly. Your knees complain. You win anyway, not because you felt like it, but because you refused to give the excuse a full hearing. By the time your brain drafted a better rebuttal, you were already back with coffee in hand and the day started.

At work you notice the same courtroom opening — too busy, too late, not the right mood for this email. You cut that too. Send the message. Make the call. No debate. The excuses do not disappear entirely, but without the feed they get quieter, and action gets easier to reach.

## Takeaway

You do not need to win a philosophical argument with your own resistance every morning. You need to recognize the excuse early and shut it down before it builds a case. Starve the negotiation and what remains is simple: the next small action you already know you should take.
