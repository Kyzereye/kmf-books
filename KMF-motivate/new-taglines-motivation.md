## 01. Done Beats Perfect - motivation

*Done Beats Perfect*

A finished rough draft moves you forward; a perfect plan that never ships keeps you standing still. Stop polishing and start delivering — done is the only version that counts.

## 10. Move Before Ready - motivation

*Move Before Ready*

Ready is a feeling that shows up after you start, not before — move anyway and let readiness catch up to action. Quit waiting for the green light from your emotions; beginning badly beats preparing forever.

## 15. Own The Morning - motivation

*Own The Morning*

Morning is when excuses are shortest — own the first hour before the day owns you. Win that window and the rest has something real to follow.

## 19. Speak Less Do More - motivation

*Speak Less Do More*

Talk is cheap and everyone has plans — let your actions be so loud they drown out what you said you'd do. Your world doesn't need another announcement; it needs your execution.

## 32. Do The Hard Thing - motivation

*Do The Hard Thing*

There's always an easy option and a right option — they're rarely the same. Do the hard thing first and everything after it gets lighter.

## 34. Eat The Frog - motivation

*Eat The Frog*

Do the worst thing on your list first — the one you've been avoiding, dreading, or pretending isn't there. Swallow it early and the rest of your day tastes better.

## 42. Lead With Action - motivation

*Lead With Action*

Don't wait for permission, consensus, or perfect conditions — move first and let others follow the example. That's when talk turns into progress.

## 44. Make Momentum Your Friend - motivation

*Make Momentum Your Friend*

Momentum is built, not found — start small, stay consistent, and let the snowball do what willpower can't sustain alone. Once it's rolling, get out of its way.

## 51. Stop Rehearsing Disaster - motivation

*Stop Rehearsing Disaster*

Your mind runs the worst version on a loop — make the call, send the message, and let reality replace the fiction. Most of what you dread never shows up; the rest you can handle when there are real issues.

## 55. Choose The Hard Door - motivation

*Choose The Hard Door*

Two doors — one easy, one hard. The easy door keeps you comfortable and stuck; walk through the hard one and the day changes shape.

## 61. Run The First Lap - motivation

*Run The First Lap*

The whole race is too much to face from a standing start — run the first lap and commit to nothing more. Get moving and momentum will carry what willpower couldn't.

## 62. Open The Document - motivation

*Open The Document*

Preparation feels like progress until nothing lands on the page — open the document and put the cursor where the work actually lives. One open file and thinking stops masquerading as writing.

## 65. Make The Call First - motivation

*Make The Call First*

The call you're avoiding is usually the logjam. Make it first and watch the rest of your list flow.
