# Take The First Shift

*Take The First Shift*

Somebody has to take the first shift on the hard work — let it be you today, before comfort talks you out of it.

## Category

**Motivation** — Hard work gets deferred because everyone assumes someone else will go first; *Take The First Shift* answers "How do I get started when I don't feel like it?" by volunteering for the opening stretch before comfort rewrites the plan.


## Theme

**Push Through** — Taking the first shift on hard work before comfort talks you out is the push-through move of showing up first.
## The idea

Difficult projects often stall at the threshold because the first stretch is the least comfortable — no momentum yet, no proof it will work, no one else who has broken the ice. Everyone waits for someone to take the first shift, and the waiting itself becomes the culture. When you take that shift yourself, you stop negotiating with the easier version of the day. You absorb the awkward beginning so the work can exist, and you give everyone else — including future you — something to build on.

## Story

The community garden plot had been weedy and neglected since spring. The organizer sent emails. People liked the posts. Nobody showed up on Saturday mornings because the first sessions meant thorns, heat, and hauling broken tools from the shed.

Derek almost skipped again. His back hurt. His coffee was good. He told himself someone more energetic would eventually handle it. Then he thought about how many Saturdays he had already spent telling himself that.

He drove over at eight with gloves and a shovel he owned. For ninety minutes he cleared one bed alone. His shirt was soaked. A neighbor walking a dog stopped and asked what he was doing. By nine-thirty she had gone home for gloves. By ten, two more people were pulling weeds beside him.

Nobody thanked him with a speech. They just worked. By noon the bed was ready for planting, and the group had a date for next week that did not feel hypothetical anymore.

Derek did not become the permanent leader. He became the person who took the first shift when the project needed a body in the dirt. That was the whole difference between a good idea on email and a garden that existed.

## Takeaway

When everyone is waiting for someone else to begin, that is your signal. Take the first uncomfortable stretch today, before comfort talks you into another week of standing on the sidelines.
